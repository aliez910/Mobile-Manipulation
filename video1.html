AISMPC: Adaptive Integral Sliding Mode Predictive Controller for Trajectory Tracking of Mobile Manipulator

Mobile manipulators combine the mobility of mobile platforms with the precision of manipulators, offering significant potential for flexible industrial automation. However, their high degrees of freedom present significant challenges for precise trajectory tracking in constrained environments. To address the challenge, we present AISMPC, a novel whole-body motion controller that decouples disturbance rejection from trajectory tracking. Specifically, we augment a nominal Model Predictive Controller (MPC) with an adaptive Integral Sliding Mode (ISMC) auxiliary loop. This auxiliary controller is formulated as a Quadratic Programming (QP) problem to ensure real-time responsiveness. Simulation and real-world experiments demonstrate that our controller achieves superior tracking accuracy than baseline methods, without compromising the performance required to maintain a 10Hz real-time control rate. We also integrate our method with the visual servoing system to enhance mobile manipulation task performance, boosting the success rate by 24% and reducing the placement error by 46%.
